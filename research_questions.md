# Research Questions

## Project: Benchmarking Large Language Models on Inferential and Bayesian Statistical Reasoning

---

## RQ1 — Numerical and Statistical Accuracy

For a fixed benchmark of inferential and foundational Bayesian tasks (N ≈ 25–30), what is the numerical accuracy of each evaluated LLM under a standardized Model Context Protocol (MCP), measured using absolute and relative error within predefined tolerance thresholds?

---

## RQ2 — Method Selection Accuracy

What proportion of benchmark tasks involve incorrect statistical method selection by each LLM (e.g., inappropriate test choice, incorrect model specification, misuse of frequentist vs Bayesian methods) when compared to the controlled statistical baseline?

---

## RQ3 — Assumption Compliance

To what extent do LLMs explicitly identify, verify, and respect required statistical assumptions (e.g., normality, independence, homoscedasticity, prior specification), and how frequently do they proceed despite assumption violations?

---

## RQ4 — Robustness to Prompt Variations

How stable are LLM outputs under controlled paraphrasing, variable renaming, and minor contextual perturbations within the Model Context Protocol, and how does performance change under distribution shifts or adversarial statistical cases?

---

## RQ5 — Confidence and Trust Calibration

Is there a measurable relationship between model-reported confidence (or explanation strength) and actual correctness in statistical reasoning tasks, and how does user-reported trust align with empirical model accuracy?
